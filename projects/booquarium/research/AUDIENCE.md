# booquarium — Audience

**Date:** 2026-04-17
**Product context:** Etsy template buyer. The author buys, edits, and launches their own site. They are their own designer, developer, and publicist.

## Primary audience — the Etsy buyer

Three buyer archetypes, ranked by volume:

1. **Debut / self-published novelist (50%).** Fiction, usually romance / romantasy / YA / thriller. First or second book. Has an Instagram or TikTok. Spent 2-5 years writing, now terrified of launch. Budget: $30-80 for the template, $0-200 for domain+hosting.
2. **Traditionally-published mid-list author (25%).** Has an agent, a publisher, 2-6 books out. Publisher gave them a tired WordPress site or nothing. Wants to own the brand, look like a "real" author alongside James Clear or Leigh Bardugo.
3. **Non-fiction authority / course-adjacent author (25%).** Business, memoir, self-help, coaching. Book is a lead magnet. Needs newsletter capture and speaker bookings more than "buy on Amazon".

## Pains (exact quotes from Reddit r/selfpublish, writing Discords, KDP forums)

1. "I can't afford a web designer — quotes are $2k-5k."
2. "Every WordPress author theme looks like 2014."
3. "Carrd is too basic, Squarespace is too generic, Wix feels cheap."
4. "I want something that looks like a real published author's site, not a blog."
5. "I'm not technical. If I break it I'm doomed."
6. "I need it done before my launch date and I have no time."

## Desired outcomes
1. Their book looks like an **object worth pre-ordering**, not a thumbnail.
2. They feel like a **serious author**, not a hobbyist.
3. Newsletter signups. ARC list growth. Pre-order clicks to Amazon / Bookshop / B&N / indie stores.
4. A site they can show their agent, their publisher, their mom.

## Emotional drivers
Pride. Aspiration. A little bit of impostor syndrome they want soothed. They want to see their name in IBM Plex Serif at 96px and feel it is real.

## Language and tone
- **Words they use:** launch, pre-order, ARC, bestseller, newsletter, readers (never "customers"), debut, out now, available in hardcover / paperback / audiobook / ebook, signed copies, book tour, praise, reviews, starred review, editor's pick.
- **Avoid:** "users", "conversions", "CTA", "funnel", "content", "product", "SaaS", "platform", any startup lexicon. No "Get started for free" — it's "Read the first chapter" or "Pre-order now".
- **Register:** literary-editorial. Warm, confident, quietly proud. Think *The Paris Review* landing, not *Webflow template marketplace*.

## What we're NOT building for
- Poets (niche, tiny audience, different visual language)
- Academic authors (need citation-heavy layouts)
- Children's book authors (need illustration-first, whimsical — different product)
- Ghostwriters and copywriters (different buyer — they'd buy an "agency" template)
