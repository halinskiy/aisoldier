# Template-Design — Audience

**Date:** 2026-04-11
**Derived from:** Footer copy in the Figma Style Guide + visible content in the Home screenshot.

## Primary audience
- **Who they are:** HNW / UHNW individual investors, family office principals, institutional allocators evaluating a fiduciary wealth manager.
- **Industry:** Financial advisory, SEC-regulated.
- **Company stage:** Target firm is positioning as a fiduciary-first advisor — likely an established RIA, not a startup.
- **Budget they control:** $1M-$100M+ in investable assets (individual HNW to institutional scale).

## Pain points
1. Fatigue with opaque fee structures and conflict-of-interest commissions at large incumbents.
2. Distrust of generic "wealth management" marketing that feels like it could belong to any bank.
3. Wanting a single advisor they can actually reach, not a rotating help-desk.
4. Compliance-minded: want visible SEC registration and transparent disclosures, not hidden in a footer link.

## Desired outcomes
1. Clear signal that this firm is fiduciary (legally obligated to act in client interest) — not a broker.
2. Understand the process: onboarding, ongoing relationship, reporting cadence.
3. See evidence of discipline and track record without overstated promises (SEC rules forbid).
4. Book a consultation that doesn't feel like a sales funnel.

## Emotional drivers
**Before visit:** skeptical, fatigued by generic finance marketing, budget-rich but time-poor.
**After visit:** calm, informed, convinced this firm respects their intelligence. Book the call.

## Language and tone
- **Words they use:** fiduciary, disciplined, transparent, fee-only, evidence-based, long-horizon, principal, allocation, drawdown, compliance.
- **Words we should avoid:** "hustle", "game-changer", "AI-powered", "democratize", "crushing it", "10x", any crypto language.
- **Register:** editorial, considered, confident without bombast. Short declarative sentences. Long-form paragraphs only where we earn them.

## What we're NOT building for
- Retail investors looking for Robinhood-tier trading.
- Crypto-curious speculators.
- Small-business bookkeeping.
- Job seekers (no careers page in default scope).
