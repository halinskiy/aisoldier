The Author

Before words, there were maps.

Elena Voss studied cartography at the University of Edinburgh before she understood that she was really studying how people decide what to leave out. She spent a decade making maps of places that no longer exist. The Cartographer's Daughter is her first novel.

Inside the Book

Four things worth reading for.

Inside01
01

A house full of secrets

  1. Chapter 01

    A house full of secrets

    When Mara inherits her estranged father's Edinburgh flat, she finds 300 hand-drawn maps covering every wall except one.

  2. Chapter 02

    Two timelines, one truth

    The novel moves between 1970s Leningrad and present-day Scotland, tracing what a single act of erasure costs across generations.

  3. Chapter 03

    Language as landscape

    Voss writes sentences the way a surveyor lays out ground: precise, unhurried, and always in service of the full picture.

  4. Chapter 04

    No easy resolutions

    This is a novel that trusts its readers enough to leave certain borders undrawn.
